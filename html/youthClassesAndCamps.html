Youth Classes and Camps

Our youth classes and camps are designed to get kids ages 8-18 learning STEM concepts and applying this knowledge with hands-on projects.

Youth Camps

These multi-day camps teach complex ideas that build upon each other.

Woodworking Camp

Dates: November 3-December 15,2020

Instructor: Eric Cavazos-Smith

This introduction to the world of industrial design through woodworking will enable both new and experienced woodworkers alike to develop modern woodworking skills, safely in our community woodshop.

Students will get hands-on experience using a mini-lathe to turn a wooden pen, a planer in the finishing process of making a laminated edge grain cutting board, a bandsaw, and drill press to build an analog clock, and a combination of those tools to make a wooden toolbox.

In the process of building multiple projects, we'll learn modern wood preparation including measuring, jointer, planer, drum sander, routing, lathe, cutting, glue techniques, sanding, and the finishing process in this action-packed weekly camp.

Cost: $625

Textiles Camp

Dates: Wednesdays September 30-October 28, 2020

Instructor: Carmen Almlie-Martinez

Oh snap! Are you thready for 6-weeks of building spooky textile projects? In addition to sewing costumes we will learn:

  • Interpreting and making your own patterns;
  • Fabric anatomy, including warp, bias, weft, selvage, fold, and understanding fabric such as knit vs. woven;
  • Heat-transfer vinyl cutting to heat press customized designs onto projects we create;
  • Direct to garment printing to print any design we like onto the fabric we'll use to sew
Students with both beginner and advanced sewing skills alike will benefit from this camp. Sew like you mean it!

In addition to planned projects, students will come up with their own project and make it. Halloween is approaching fast - any ghouly, gaudy or scary costumes and props to go with them can be brought to life in this class.

Cost: $425

Youth Classes

These single-day classes give kids and teens great hands-on experience

Sew an Apron

Date: October 10, 2020

Instructor: Sarah Kramer

Learn to sew by making an apron! We will customize the designs, cut out our pieces, learn to serge the edges, add pockets, and finish our pieces. Participants will also learn the workings of a sewing machine, including winding bobbins and threading the machine.

You will learn how to:

  • interpret a pattern
  • select and layout materials
  • cut, iron, and pin your pieces
  • thread a sewing machine and wind a bobbin
  • perform basic stitches with a sewing machine
  • finish edges and seams
All tools and materials will be provided. We have plenty of patterns, however, participants are welcome to bring their own natural fiber (cotton preferred) fabric.

Cost: $90
